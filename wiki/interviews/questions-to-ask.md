# Smart Questions to Ask Interviewers

> Last updated: 2026-04-12 | Shows you're thinking, not just answering

## About the Team

1. "What's the team's on-call rotation like? How are incidents handled?"
   → Shows I care about operational maturity. Also tells me if it's a sweatshop.

2. "How does knowledge sharing work on the team? Wikis, runbooks, documentation culture?"
   → Shows I value documentation. Also tells me if tribal knowledge is a problem.

3. "What's the biggest operational challenge the team faced in the last 6 months?"
   → Tells me what I'll actually be working on. Also shows curiosity.

4. "How does the team decide between 'build vs buy' for tooling?"
   → Shows systems thinking. Also tells me if they invest in engineering.

5. "What's the ratio of new feature work to operational/maintenance work?"
   → Tells me if I'll be building or just keeping the lights on.

## About the Technical Stack

6. "What's the ML model deployment pipeline like? From training to production?"
   → Shows MLOps interest. Tells me if they have real ML infrastructure.

7. "How do you monitor model drift and performance in production?"
   → Shows I know ML in production is different from ML in notebooks.

8. "What's the Kubernetes setup like? Managed or self-hosted? Multi-cluster?"
   → Shows K8s expertise. Gives me signal on infrastructure maturity.

9. "How are secrets managed? Service principals or managed identity?"
   → Shows security awareness. Tells me if they follow best practices.

10. "What's the CI/CD pipeline technology? How long from commit to production?"
    → Shows DevOps thinking. Tells me about deployment velocity.

## About the Role

11. "What does success look like in the first 3 months for this role?"
    → Shows I want to deliver value quickly. Sets clear expectations.

12. "Is there a patent program? Does the company support IP development?"
    → Shows innovation mindset. Tells me about intellectual property culture.

13. "What's the career growth path for this role? What did the previous person in this role go on to do?"
    → Shows ambition. Tells me if people grow here or leave.

14. "Are there opportunities to present at conferences or publish papers?"
    → Shows thought leadership interest. Tells me about learning culture.

15. "What's the team's relationship with other engineering teams? Collaborative or siloed?"
    → Shows I care about cross-team dynamics. Tells me about org health.

## Reverse-Interview Power Moves

16. "I noticed [company] is using [technology X]. I've been exploring [technology Y] for [similar use case]. Have you considered it?"
    → Shows I've done homework on the company AND have technical depth.

17. "In my current role, I built [knowledge graph / carrier intelligence system]. Is there a similar need here?"
    → Shows I create value proactively. Plants the seed that I'm a builder.

18. "What's one thing you'd change about the current infrastructure if you had unlimited resources?"
    → Tells me the real pain points. Shows I think about improvement.

19. "How does the engineering team learn from incidents? Post-mortems? Blameless culture?"
    → Shows SRE maturity. Tells me if they're a learning org.

20. "What's the most exciting technical challenge you think this team will tackle in the next year?"
    → Shows I'm looking ahead. Tells me if the future work excites me.

## Questions That Signal Seniority

21. "What's the tech debt situation? Is there time allocated for paying it down?"
    → Senior engineers care about sustainability.

22. "How are architecture decisions made? RFC process? Design reviews?"
    → Senior engineers want to influence architecture.

23. "What's the observability stack? Do you have distributed tracing?"
    → Senior engineers know observability is critical.

24. "How do you handle multi-region or DR? Active-active or active-passive?"
    → Senior engineers think about resilience at scale.

25. "What's the budget process for infrastructure? Can teams self-serve?"
    → Senior engineers think about efficiency and autonomy.
